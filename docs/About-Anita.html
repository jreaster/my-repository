"Anita"

The following description of Anita’s prolific artistic career is written by her husband. It is therefore biased, but rightly so, as you will appreciate by perusing the artwork showcased on this website. She works in a wide range of mediums; ink on paper, charcoal pencil on paper, watercolor and acrylic paint on paper and canvas, collage on paper and canvas, and collage on three dimensional objects ( miniature houses for example), sweet potato stamps on paper, and photography.

The topics she portrays in her various media are equally varied, including;

(1) Darling ink on paper drawings of the athletic endeavors and ensuing emotions of young girls and women, and stunning portraits of senior women,

(2) Charcoal pencil drawings of endearing kittens in various poses and predicaments, and husbands and wives in everyday poses,

(3) Watercolor and acrylic whimsical portals of subjects that vary from shoes to animals,

(4) Collages that capture the eye for a through inspection of the piece,

(5) Photography with a talent to spot and focus one’s attention to details that get missed in every day experiences,

(6) A magnificent installation portraying the American Stars and Strips flag the is eleven (11) feet tall and seventeen (17) feet wide. Consisting of 221 individual photos taken near Mojave California arranged in such as manner as to appear as the American flag when viewed from a distance.
